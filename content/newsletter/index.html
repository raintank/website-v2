March 2016 Newsletter

Hi,
It's hard to believe we're already three months into 2016. Rather than do a stuffy newsletter, we asked the team what they've been most excited about and want to share:

Grafana 3.0

I'm very excited that Grafana 3.0 is almost ready! The weeks of 18hr days refactoring code were tough, but the beta is planned to be released in the next few weeks.

A key improvement is a totally revamped plugin architecture -- we want to make it easy for the community to discover and create new Data Sources, Panels, Dashboards, and even entire Applications for Grafana.

  

Torkel Ödegaard
Co-founder, Creator of Grafana

Litmus is now:

We've spent a lot of time this year thinking about our product names, and our Litmus app is being renamed to worldPing. We think it is a better, and more self descriptive name for monitoring global network performance.

With the upcoming Grafana 3.0 release, you'll be able to install worldPing (and a slew of other upcoming apps) into your existing Grafana.

  

Raj Dutt
Co-founder

New metrics database: better dashboard performance.

We switched from KairosDB to a new time series database for Graphite on top of Cassandra. You will see major improvements in load times and availability, though it's not yet where we want to be and the next few months will bring more. Load a dashboard to see for yourself. we're anxious to hear your feedback. Currently, five weeks of historical data is available, and we plan to raise that in the future.

For those interested, all code is on Github and keep an eye out for a technical blog post with more details.

Between the 17th-21st of February we were scaling out the platform and ran into some into some issues. We couldn't do it without some data loss, so you may see some gaps during that timeframe. I'm sorry about that. We're working on raising the bar and when we become GA we'll have proper incident and status communication in place.

  

Dieter Plaetinck
Principal Engineer, metrics 2.0 rockstar

Bring worldPing data into your Grafana

I've talked to a lot of raintank early access users of the last few months, and people either love this feature or don't know about it yet.

By adding the raintank datasource to your existing Grafana, you can visualize and correlate worldPing data with your own. It only takes a minute. Here's how.

  

Matt Toback
VP Customer Experience, Visiting Perth and thinks it's awesome

Assorted worldPing improvements

We've spruced up the Event dashboard. Thanks to the new table panel in Grafana, it now has proper filtering and sorting support. We've also made significant improvements in our backend event database.

The Ping dashboard has also been redesigned to better convey latency and packet loss.

We've also added custom timeout controls for individual Ping, DNS, HTTP and HTTPS checks. Add a new endpoint or configure an existing endpoint to check it out.

  

Anthony Woods
Co-founder, lives in Perth. Doesn't think it's as exciting as Matt does

Design and UI Enhancements

As proud as we are of our interface design, there's always room for improvement. In the upcoming Grafana 3.0 release, as well as the worldPing interface, you'll see that we've taken great care in making the UI more clean, intuitive and beautiful for both the light and dark themes.

We've been designing additional interface icons, choosing new fonts that are readable even at small sizes, and cleaned up the color palette so the interface can get out of your way, and your data can take center stage. And we're not stopping there; many more improvements and surprises are in store over the coming weeks.

  

Trent White
VP, Marketing and Design

As always, a sincere thank you for the continued feedback and support during our early access period. Talk soon!
